Government Website Redesign

Case Study

US Forest Service

Designer: Sam Lynch

Product

The United States Forest Service (USFS) is an agency of the U.S. Department of Agriculture that administers the nation's 154 national forests and 20 national grasslands.

Challenge

The website for the US Forest Service has a vast amount of information on a wide variety of topics. Since the site features so much different content, the navigation is complicated and the design is clunky and inconsistent.

Solution

Design, prototype, test, code and launch a fully functional and responsive website. The new site will help direct new customers and grow the business.

Research

User Persona

Since the Forest Service website covers a wide variety of topics, a persona was created to narrow down the scope of the redesign.

Heuristic Evaluation

An evaluation was run on the run on the Forest Service website using Nielsen’s 10 general principles for interaction design.

Issues to Solve First

  • Inconsistent header hierarchy
  • Lack of primary goal or call to action
  • Inconsistent white space
  • Content takes up too much of page
  • Muted color palette with dark browns and greens.
  • No 404 page exists

Testing

A/B Testing

Prototypes for two navigation layouts were created, a top navigation and a side navigation. A quick user was conducted with 5 different subjects. to determine the most effective navigation to use.

Usability Testing

Usability tests were conducted with 8 subjects (4 desktop, 4 mobile) in order to discover design issues, navigation painpoints and areas for improvement

Test Goals

  • Can users find secondary and teritary pages in the navigation?
  • Do users understand their location on the site at any given point?
  • What calls to action are users most likely to focus on?
  • Is the user’s experience enjoyable while viewing the site on mobile?
  • How are users typically navigating around the site? (breadcrumbs, main navigation, clicking links etc.)

Takeaways

  • Breadcrumbs should stick lock position along with top/side navigation
  • Breadcrumbs are useful but should be larger on mobile
  • Theres a lot of whitespace on the sides for the mobile design
  • Users are looking to the navigation first to find a page
  • Features and news pages should have way to filter content

Conclusion

Takeaways

  • The largest challenge of this project was to redesign the site's complex navigation in a way that improves the user’s experience without losing any relevant information. Conducting several user tests on different navigation designs helped guide these decisions.
  • Because of the timeframe, I knew I would be unable to redesign every page on the website. I relied on my user research and interviews to narrow down the scope of the redesign and focus content relevant to my user persona.

Next Steps

  1. Continue to iterate and test the find a forest feature and sub pages.
  2. Build other pages relevant to user personas such as employment opportunities and publications.
  3. Build all remaining secondary pages and run additional user tests on the IA, UI and navigation.
